The titleof this project is

Error Bound on Shor's Algorithm on a superconducting setup || Supervisor: Prof. Ujjwal Sen, HRI

Duration: August'21-Present

  • Here, I am working on Shor’s algorithm while factoring small numbers, and quantifying error in a superconducting setup, the algorithm was actually performed at UCSB with a 9 element quantum processor with N = 15 and a = 4.
  • We use different composite numbers( e.g 15, 21, 35 etc.), with same noise(given) and different co-primes, to find a bound on error that can affect the success rate of the algorithm, depending on the corresponding Quantum circuit.
  • We focus on specific quantum noisy channels that allow us to consider single qubit relaxation time and dephasing time and check how much the success rate diverges from the ideal case(≥ 50%). Using the noisy channel the fidelity of the final density reduces polinomially with time to perform the experiment.
