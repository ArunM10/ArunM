The titleof this project is

Advancing Quantum Metrological accuracy for Bound Entangled States || Supervisor: Prof. Leong-Chuan Kwek, CQT

Duration: September'20-March'21

  • Quantum metrology overcomes standard precision limits and has the potential to play a key role in quantum sensing. Conventional bounds to measurement precision such as the shot-noise limit are not as fundamental as the Heisenberg limits and can be beaten with quantum strategies that employ ‘quantum tricks’ such as squeezing and entanglement. Bipartite entangled quantum states with positive partial transpose (PPT), are usually considered to be too weakly entangled for applications.
  • We provided a new strategy to estimate unknown parameters, in the field of Quantum Metrology, rather than the mainstream Entangled assisted strategy which can outperform the latter in terms of accuracy.
  • We were able to outperform the conventional Heisenberg limit, with a scaling of O(n^2) in QFI.
  • We further obtained the effect of noise inside the system using specific noisy channel.
  • Our work has been published in an international journal as well as selected in many international conferences.
