The titleof this project is

Master's Thesis || Supervisors: Prof. Leong-Chuan Kwek, CQT and Prof. Bibhas Adhikari, IITKGP

Duration: September'21-Present

  • My master’s thesis also involves the application of VQA but now on a disordered spin-glass system. Quantum spin glass is an array of spins where the alignment of these spins are not fixed because of the frustrating nature of the lattice. Interaction terms belong to random Gaussian distribution, and this randomness in the alignment leads to unstable ground state energy.
  • We analyzed the behavior of the ground state energy w.r.t the variance and mean of the distribution with the help of three probleminspired ansatz.
  • We observed the entanglement (ENT) spectra of these ansatz and all of them having spectra close to the MP (Marchenko-Pastur) distribution which concludes that they don’t have restricted manifold thus should be the best choice for optimization. But the reason for not reaching the exact ground state energy for some of the ansatz actually lies in the concept of expressibility and overparameterization and ENT spectra alone can’t be sufficient to provide enough pieces of evidence.
  • We explicitly showed the multidimensional cost landscape for under and overparameterization. We are also doing the analytic study on expressibility of random unitary drawn from Haar measure as well as the application of QFI in that context.
