The titleof this project is

Quantum Algorithm & Communication || Supervisor: Prof. Bibhas Adhikari, IITKGP

Duration: Sept'19-Jan'20 & May'20-Aug'20

  • Worked on a project based on Perfect state transfer(PST). Reproduced the basic results of PST from different articles e.g. time taken to transfer some state, with fidelity close to 1, from a particular node of a chain or a hypercube to another node of the respective graph structure. Also explored graph theory and applications to apply the idea of PST on various forms of graphs. PST can also be used for quantum communication purposes as well as to build large scale quantum computers.
  • Involved in a project on Multi particle state overlap (including entangles states) which can describe the upper bound to the noise to any nearby Qudit (d>2) for state transfer or any communication network protocol. Considering a 3- qubit chain system, while the overlap between the adjacent qubits are known. Now, we can obtain a bound of the overlap between the 1st and 3rd qubit and thats how we can quantify how far the 1st qubit is from the 3rd one. We extended it to an entangled setup and found different bounds. Also we tried to extend it to noisy system but at the end the results were not enough to publish.
